image 1: the mural (2025) depicts a collective vision for nature in mabopane, south africa, and was designed over multiple workshops with local residents, secondary school learners, and university researchers. indigenous plants and animals, connection to ancestors, clean water, community action, education, and safe access to nature-- these all became emerging pieces to the puzzle.

funding and support provided by collaboration on nature-based solutions for sustainable cities (consus) and the danida fellowship centre.

image 2: our quirky team at opening day from left to right; brenda ramadiehe (mural artist), lebo phala (project co-facilitator), myself (project leader), simon leeuw (volunteer), masego sesana (project co-facilitator), thabiso jeffrey maphuphuma (volunteer). the shadow at the bottom is kathy berger (ph.d. student and project co-facilitator) documenting this moment.

ps. “leamogetswe” is tswana for “welcome”
